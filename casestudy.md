# The Case Study: Arjun's Chaotic Monday

## Meet Arjun Mehta

Arjun is the VP of Product at a Series B B2B SaaS company in Koramangala, Bangalore. The company builds an operations platform for mid-market businesses — think dashboards, reporting, workflow automation. About 100 employees, growing fast, burning cash, and trying to prove unit economics to a board that includes Sequoia India and Accel.

Arjun manages a team of 12 across product, design, and research. He reports to Vikram, the CEO. His key stakeholders are Rohit (Platform), Suresh (Engineering), Priya (his sharpest PM), and Anika (Legal/Ops — who is becoming a bottleneck on every contract).

**Arjun is good at his job. He's also drowning.**

---

## What's on Arjun's Plate (Q1 2025)

### 1. Project Alpha — The Big Bet
Self-serve onboarding for SMB customers. Right now, onboarding takes 2 weeks and requires a Customer Success Manager to hold the customer's hand. The goal: reduce it to 2 days, zero-touch. The board is asking about unit economics — the cost of a CSM per SMB customer simply doesn't work at scale.

Meera (designer/researcher) found that 60% of SMB customers want video walkthroughs, not documentation. That changes the scope and the budget.

### 2. Acme Corp — The Account That Can't Be Lost
Arjun's largest client. INR 45 lakhs annually. Deepak Sharma, VP Operations at Acme, is a straight-talker. He likes the platform. His ops team saves 8 hours a week on reporting. But the mobile app crashes 2-3 times a day for field agents, and CompetitorX just quoted Acme 30% less on the enterprise tier.

Contract expires March 31. Deepak has said plainly: show progress on mobile and pricing by mid-February, or they start migration planning. No drama, no threats — just the reality.

### 3. Board Meeting — March 20
Anjali Desai (Sequoia India) will push on pricing. Ravi Krishnan (ex-Flipkart CTO, independent board member) will want the real technical picture on self-serve. Nandini Rao (Accel) cares about market size. Vikram's guidance: "Come with data, not opinions."

---

## Arjun's Real Problem

It's not that Arjun doesn't know what to do. He's been doing this for 15 years. The problem is that his knowledge is **scattered across 20+ files** — meeting notes, email drafts, Slack threads, budget spreadsheets, to-do lists, half-finished analyses. Some in Notion, some in random text files, some in his head.

He knows the Acme renewal is critical. He knows the board wants pricing data. He knows Priya is overworked and Suresh's team has competing priorities. But connecting these dots — seeing the full picture — requires him to open 8 tabs, cross-reference 5 documents, and hold the entire system in his head.

**That's what you're about to fix.**

## Why This Case Study Works for YOU

No matter your role, Arjun's situation will feel familiar:

**If you're a founder or CEO** — You've lived the board prep anxiety, the customer retention fire, the team stretched too thin. Your version of this chaos might be investor updates, hiring pipelines, and product decisions all living in different places.

**If you're in sales or BD** — Notice the Acme Corp thread. A INR 45 lakh account at risk, an ROI email half-drafted, pricing pressure from a competitor. You've written that email. You've had that client call.

**If you're a consultant** — Look at how many deliverables are in progress but not finished. The competitor analysis is a draft. The ROI comparison needs verification. The proposal is expiring. Sound familiar?

**If you're in marketing** — The newsletter draft, the customer communication about webhook changes, the positioning against CompetitorX. Content and messaging scattered across files, waiting to be pulled together.

**If you're in finance or operations** — The budget is over in month one. Expense reports are pending. A INR 25 lakh vendor contract is about to expire. Revenue data exists but nobody has synthesized the story for the board.

**If you're in HR or people management** — Priya is burning out. New hires need onboarding. A senior role is open but the JD isn't posted. Performance reviews are overdue.

**The point:** Arjun's mess isn't about product management. It's about being a senior professional with too many threads and no system to hold them together. That's the problem you're about to solve — first for Arjun, then for yourself.
